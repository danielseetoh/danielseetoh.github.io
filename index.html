Tyrian Mock

Simple 2D game with HTML5 and Javascript

Try it out!

This was a small project of mine based on the old classic Tyrian. I used to really enjoy playing the simple arcade-style spaceship shooter game, so I decided to create a similar version to it. This game uses HTML5 canvas and Javascript, to achieve a simple but neat interface. This was the first time that I developed something in HTML5 canvas as well as worked with sprites, and I really enjoyed the experience.

The game uses graphics and audio from the original Tyrian, which was made available to the public in 2007. It currently features enemy ships, power ups and enemy bosses. Beware of enemy bullets as well as collisions with enemy planes. Have fun racking up your score!

Convex Hull with Jarvis' March

This demo showcases basic Jarvis' March, also known as the gift wrapping algorithm, for finding the convex hull of a series of points. You are able to add in your own points as well.

SeetohBlog

A lightweight blogger for everyday use

This isn't just a blog, it's a blogger for anyone to create a blog! SeetohBlog was born from the idea of jotting down my thoughts and musings, as well as creating updates of my life. I've personally never been a blogger, but I realised that in addition to recording my learning experiences, I could take the chance to develop a service for the community by creating an easy blogging interface, and in the process learn even more!

Enter SeetohBlog! This lightweight blogger was created using python, django, postgresql, bootstrap, AWS S3(for static and media files), and heroku for hosting. My heroku account is currently on free tier, so there may be occasional times of the month where the website would not be up.

Users are able to create an account and choose their own blog name, create/edit/delete posts, respond to comments on their posts, comment on other people's posts, view other people's blogs, change their blog banners and background colors, add images to their posts etc. The blog will be created for them automatically and they can then share it with the world!

The process of creating this blogger was a very enriching one, in which I familiarised myself with a new framework (django), learned about the shortcomings of heroku(issues of storing static/media files), adopted best practices for serving such files as well as security measures that django provides, and the differences between development and production environments. The journey was not very smooth, but the couple of weeks spent on this project was well worth the experience. As a result, I now have my very own blog that I will update regularly at this site!

GrizzlyGoals

A goal tracker with social media aspects

GrizzlyGoals is a website that helps you track your goals. It came from the realization that most people have trouble sticking to their New Year's resolutions. We want to tackle the big goals at the start of the year, and this platform provides you the tools to track your progress towards these goals.

There is the idea of radical accountability, where your friends can see the goals you've created for yourself, and see the progress you're making towards them. For goals which are more sensitive, you can make them private so only you can view them.

You can also see and comment on your friends' goals, and if you see a goal that someone has made for themselves that you want to take on, you can easily duplicate their goal to your own page.

This website was made using Java, Spring Boot, Typescript, React, BlueprintJs, MySQL, and hosted on AWS using Elastic Beanstalk, RDS, S3, CloudFront, Route53 etc.
